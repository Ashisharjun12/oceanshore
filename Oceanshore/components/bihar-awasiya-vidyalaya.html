Bihar Awasiya Vidyalaya

Bihar Awasiya Vidyalaya, also known as Bihar Residential School, is a system of residential schools established by the government of Bihar, India. These schools provide free education, accommodation, and food to children from economically weaker sections of the society. The primary objective of Bihar Awasiya Vidyalaya is to improve the literacy rate in Bihar and provide quality education to the underprivileged children who otherwise would not have access to it. These schools offer education from 6th to 12th standard in both English and Hindi medium, and the curriculum is designed to prepare students for competitive exams like IIT-JEE, NEET, and other national level entrance exams. Apart from academics, Bihar Awasiya Vidyalaya also focuses on extracurricular activities like sports, music, dance, and art to develop the overall personality of the students. The schools have well-equipped laboratories, libraries, computer rooms, and playgrounds to ensure the holistic development of the students. To get admission in Bihar Awasiya Vidyalaya, students have to clear an entrance exam conducted by the Bihar School Examination Board...

Bihar Awasiya Vidyalaya

Bihar Awasiya Vidyalaya was established by the government of Bihar in the year 2009. The objective behind establishing these residential schools was to provide free education, accommodation, and food to children from economically weaker sections of the society who are unable to access quality education due to financial constraints...

Information regarding admission based on possible vacancies for Scheduled Castes and Scheduled Tribes students in all 85 residential schools in Bihar is given here. Students belonging to the Scheduled Castes and Scheduled Tribes categories can apply for classes 1 and 6. All information related to admission is given below..

How to Apply for Admission?

First of all, read the official notice which has been published on the official website - http://scstwelfare.bih.nic.in/. (The direct link to the official notice is given in the Important Link section.)

The prescribed form can be obtained from the district welfare office of the district, and the same will be submitted there after filling out the form.

Along with the prescribed form, the following documents need to be attached - a certificate of caste issued by the Anchal Officer, the original/certified copy of the educational qualification certificate, three photographs, age limit certificate, and an affidavit related to the residential certificate.
